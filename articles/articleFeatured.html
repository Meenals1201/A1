7 Subscriptions I Will Never Cancel (A Designer’s Unshakeable Toolkit)


-UX Century;  September 30, 2025

In our world, subscriptions are the new normal. From prototyping tools to icon libraries, our monthly expenses can look like a bloated SaaS dashboard. I recently conducted a ruthless audit of my own—cutting every service that didn't earn its keep.

But amidst the cancelations, I found the non-negotiables. The ones that are less like a line item on a budget and more like a trusted member of my creative team.

These are the seven I'll defend not just as a consumer, but as a designer who depends on them to do better work, faster and smarter.

1. Figma (Organization Tier)
The Obvious Reason: It's the industry standard for UI/UX design and collaboration.

The Real Reason: It's my team's single source of truth. The ability to create robust, centralized design systems with variables and advanced prototyping has eliminated the "which file is the latest?" chaos. It's not just a design tool; it's our collaboration hub that bridges the gap between design, development, and product. This isn't a cost; it's the foundation of our operational efficiency.

2. Spotify
The Obvious Reason: Access to millions of songs and podcasts.

The Real Reason: It's my auditory workspace designer. I have meticulously crafted playlists for every creative mode: deep-flow instrumental scores, upbeat synthwave for production work, and UX-focused podcasts for my commute. It doesn't just play music; it actively helps me curate the mental state I need to be productive. It's the most powerful tool for managing my creative focus.

3. Notion (Plus Plan)
The Obvious Reason: A flexible all-in-one workspace for notes and tasks.

The Real Reason: It's the operating system for my brain and my business. My client onboarding, project timelines, design sprint templates, and personal goal-tracking all live here. Its flexibility mirrors a designer's mindset—it can be a simple notepad or a complex, relational database for my design debt log. It brings order to the beautiful chaos of the creative process.

4. Relentless Asset Library (e.g., Icons8 or Envato Elements)
The Obvious Reason: A vast library of icons, illustrations, and templates.

The Real Reason: It's my creative velocity engine. When a client needs a high-fidelity mockup yesterday, I don't have 8 hours to illustrate custom icons. This subscription is a force multiplier that lets me bypass tedious asset creation and focus my energy on the core UX, strategy, and visual design that truly matters. It pays for itself by saving me a single afternoon per month.

5. A Robust Password Manager (1Password)
The Obvious Reason: It stores and generates secure passwords.

The Real Reason: It's the unsung hero of my creative flow. As a designer, I juggle dozens of tools, client portals, and developer handoff platforms. This subscription eliminates the dreaded "password reset" spiral that can completely derail a creative sprint. The few seconds it saves me each time add up to hours of protected, uninterrupted focus time every week.

6. Miro (Pro Plan)
The Obvious Reason: A digital whiteboard for workshops and brainstorming.

The Real Reason: It's my virtual war room for synthesis. While Figma is for precision, Miro is for the messy, beautiful beginning. It's where user journey maps, affinity diagrams, and stakeholder workshops come to life. It captures the raw, collaborative energy of an in-person session and makes it permanent, actionable, and shareable with remote teams. It's the birthplace of clarity.

7. Are.na
The Obvious Reason: A visual platform to collect and connect ideas.

The Real Reason: It's my private, algorithm-free inspiration garden. Unlike Pinterest, it's not driven by ads or trends. It's a quiet space where I can save typography pairings, unique layouts, color palettes from photography, and philosophical quotes, and slowly build connections between them. This is where I go to feed my creativity without the noise of the market. It's my secret weapon for developing a unique visual point of view.

The Common Thread
Looking at this list, the pattern is clear. The subscriptions worth keeping forever aren't just about convenience. They are about augmenting my capabilities.

They either:

Protect my focus (Spotify, 1Password)
Systematize my chaos (Figma, Notion)
Amplify my speed (Icons8, Miro)
Nurture my creativity (Are.na)

They don't just do a task; they make me a better, more efficient, and more inspired designer. And that is an ROI I'll happily pay for, every single month.
